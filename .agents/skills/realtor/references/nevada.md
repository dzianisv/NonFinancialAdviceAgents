# Nevada Rules — No Wage Tax, NRS Ch. 361 Property Tax, Washoe/Incline STR

> Source: Nevada Revised Statutes Ch. 361 (leg.state.nv.us); Washoe County STR FAQ
> (washoecounty.gov); Tax Foundation state-rate data (taxfoundation.org). Distilled from the W-2
> Real-Estate Tax Research run. **verified_on: 2026-07-13 — re-verify before use** (levy rates,
> abatement classification, and STR permit rules reset annually / by parcel).

## Wage tax: none

Nevada levies **no individual income tax on wage and salary income** [Tax Foundation state-rate data].
This is the headline draw — but see the NV≠CA-escape correction in `california.md`: it only benefits a
person who is **actually a Nevada resident/domiciliary with Nevada-sourced (or non-CA-sourced) work**.
A California resident who buys a Nevada home and keeps California residency and California-source wages
still owes California tax in full. **Owning Nevada property changes California wage-tax liability by
$0.**

## Property tax mechanics [NRS Ch. 361]

- **Assessment ratio:** property is assessed at **35% of taxable value** [NRS Ch. 361]. The tax rate
  is then applied to that assessed value.
- **Owner-occupied abatement cap:** the annual increase in tax on an **owner-occupied primary
  residence** is **capped at 3%** [NRS Ch. 361]. This produces a low effective carry (illustratively
  ~$6k–$8.5k on a normalized $1M Incline home — a modeled estimate, not a sourced fact).
- **Abatement classification caveat (load-bearing):** the **3% cap is primary-residence-specific**. An
  STR / non-owner-occupied property may fall under a **different, higher** abatement cap (commonly
  cited as up to 8% for other property). **Confirm the parcel's abatement classification** before
  quoting the 3% cap for a rental — an STR does not automatically get the owner-occupied cap.

## Short-term-rental permitting: Washoe County / Incline Village

- **Permit required first:** Washoe County (which includes **Incline Village**) requires a **permit
  before advertising or renting for fewer than 28 days** [Washoe County STR FAQ]. Operating without
  the permit is a violation, independent of any tax position.
- **HOA / CC&R gate (can override the county):** an HOA or the recorded CC&Rs — e.g. Incline Village
  General Improvement District (IVGID)-area associations — **can independently prohibit STRs** even
  where the county would permit them. The HOA/CC&R restriction is a **private** limit that the county
  permit does not cure.
- **Feasibility is the binding constraint.** If the HOA or CC&Rs ban short-term rentals, the entire
  §469 depreciation plan is **moot** regardless of the math. Verify permit **and** HOA/CC&Rs before
  presenting any STR shell. See `insurance-and-feasibility.md`.
- **Reno / other NV localities:** verify the **specific** municipal code live each run; do not assume
  Washoe rules apply elsewhere.

## Insurance: wildfire is the swing variable

- **Incline / Lake Tahoe wildfire exposure:** in high-risk wildland-urban-interface (WUI) areas,
  **standard admitted coverage is often unavailable**. **Nevada has no FAIR Plan** (unlike California),
  so the fallback for an Incline/NV home is the **excess & surplus (E&S) / surplus-lines** market, which
  is "significantly pricier." Do **not** cite a FAIR plan for a Nevada property.
- Model insurance as a **range**: illustratively **~$4,500 admitted → up to ~$25,000 E&S** wildfire —
  this single line can **reverse** Nevada's otherwise-cheapest carry. Never collapse it to a point.
  Full peril detail in `insurance-and-feasibility.md`.

## What to tell the user

- The no-wage-tax benefit is a **residency** outcome, not a property outcome — model residency
  separately (see `california.md`) and only if a real domicile change is intended.
- Quote the **3% owner-occupied cap only for a genuine primary residence**; flag the abatement
  reclassification for an STR.
- Quote insurance as a **range**; the E&S wildfire high case can flip the ranking.
- Close with the educational-not-advice + CPA/tax-attorney line.
