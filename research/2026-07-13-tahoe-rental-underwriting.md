### 4) Can you "depreciate the house cost in 5 years" to offset W-2?

That framing is incorrect.

- **Land never depreciates.**
- Building shell is generally long-life depreciation (LTR commonly 27.5 years; transient STR classification can be fact-specific and may differ; confirm with CPA).
- Cost segregation may move a portion into 5/7/15-year property; current §168(k) text supports 100% bonus treatment for qualified property under current law language (input-year rule, not eternal constant).
- W-2 offset depends on activity characterization: <=7-day average stay plus material participation can change passive treatment under §469 tests; otherwise losses are generally passive/suspended.
- §280A personal-use limits, basis/at-risk limits, and CA nonconformity to federal bonus can materially change outcomes.

### 5) Five-year-sale reality: tax benefit is often timing/deferral, not free money

A five-year hold should model, at minimum:

1. selling costs,  
2. loan payoff,  
3. §1245 recapture on cost-seg personalty,  
4. unrecaptured §1250 federal layer (up to 25%) on building depreciation,  
5. residual gain tax,  
6. suspended passive-loss release where applicable.

So accelerated depreciation can help early cash taxes but does not by itself make a weak property good.
