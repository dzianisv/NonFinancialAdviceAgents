## 1. Venue decision: STAY ON ALPACA

**Decision: stay on Alpaca Crypto. Honest all-in round-trip cost to gate against: 45–55bp BTC/ETH, 50–65bp SOL** (15bp maker / 25bp taker per side → 40bp fee floor under the bot's actual maker-entry/taker-timeout-exit fill mix, plus ~4–8bp spread BTC/ETH, ~8–15bp SOL on Alpaca's thinner retail-only book).

**Why not move:**

- **Binance.US** is the only nominally cheaper reachable venue, and it fails the gate's own requirements twice over: (a) its true all-in cost is **unverified** — multiple 2026 sources flag it as having the widest, most volatile USD-pair spreads of the major venues (residue of the 2023–2025 litigation years), so its all-in cost could exceed Alpaca's once a real spread is measured; (b) it has **no sandbox/testnet**, so the gate cycle's paper-trading/shadow step cannot run there at all. Standing rule adopted: **never switch venues on a fee schedule alone — a live L1/L2 spread audit over a representative window (incl. low-liquidity hours) is a precondition.**
- **Conflict flagged, not smoothed:** the venue research says Binance.US is 0bp maker / 2bp taker (effective 2026-04-22, marketed permanent); the fee-tier hurdle analysis says 10bp/10bp flat → 20bp RT at base tier. These two inputs disagree and were not reconciled. The decision is unchanged either way (unverified spread + no sandbox dominate), but the discrepancy must be resolved before any spread audit is scoped.
- **Kraken** at this account's real entry tier (<$50k/30d) is 32–52bp RT depending on maker/taker mix — never cheaper than Alpaca (breakeven spread is negative), contrary to its reputation. **Coinbase Advanced** at entry tier is 90–120bp RT. **Gemini** base-tier figures conflicted across sources (0.20/0.40 vs 0.60/1.20 vs 0.15/0.30) — unverified, and not competitive under any reading. **Crypto.com Exchange** and **Hyperliquid** are flatly US-unavailable. **Robinhood Crypto's** default routing is principal/spread-markup (~95bp monetized), not a restable CLOB — running this bot's maker-fill simulator against it would be a category error.
- **Fees have never been the binding constraint here.** Every named kill in this repo's history was DSR-binding or gross-edge-sign-binding, not fee-binding; the corrected DSR hurdles (1.04 @N=1 → 1.37 @N=2 → 1.80 @N=5, annualized, on the 2.5y OOS window) are fee-independent. Cheap tiers (4–10bp RT) only change the feasible region for not-yet-tried shapes at 150–3000 trades/yr, and per the fee-tier analysis only the ~20bp tier (Binance.US, single venue, unaudited) is realistically reachable — the 4/10/16bp rows are a sensitivity study, not a deployment plan.

**Cost-model/fill-sim changes required to gate honestly against Alpaca (mandatory even while staying):**

1. **Retire the legacy 30bp maker-RT assumption** (`ROADMAP.md` §3, `core/gate.py` cost params) as the headline cost. The honest Alpaca all-in figure is 45–55bp BTC/ETH under the bot's own fill model. This is not academic: several of this round's proposals priced themselves at 30bp and looked ~0.1–0.15 Sharpe better than reality. At 52 trades/yr the gross-edge floor moves 106.9bp → ~127bp; at 365/yr, 41bp → ~61–66bp.
2. **Replace search-sourced spread placeholders with measured Alpaca L1/L2 quotes.** The venue research states plainly that its spread numbers are informed placeholders, not measurements, and that the gate's cost model must not run on search-sourced spread guesses.
3. **Re-calibrate fill probability and adverse drift on Alpaca's own book.** Alpaca's CLOB is retail-only proprietary liquidity (Coinbase/Kraken feed it reference *prices*, not depth); a fill model calibrated on Kraken/Coinbase L2 history overstates fill quality there. The maker-fill assumption is validated in this repo **only** for BTC/ETH, 5m bars, patient resting orders (97–99% fills, 0.4–0.9bp adverse drift); xs_momentum's 11.7bp adverse drift shows it does not transfer to thinner assets or one-shot rebalance cadences. Do not port it to SOL or any new cadence without re-measurement.
